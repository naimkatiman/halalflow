# HalalFlow: Open-Source Built-in-Public Strategy

---

## 1. Positioning

**Core statement:**
> "HalalFlow is the open-source workflow tool for people running Islamic finance operations — built in public, week by week."

Not a platform. Not an ecosystem. A tool, built transparently, for operators who need it now.

**Why this works:**
- "Built in public" signals honesty and momentum
- "Islamic finance operations" is specific enough to repel the wrong audience
- "Open-source" lowers the trust barrier immediately
- You're not competing with Temenos or Mambu — you're serving the operator who has a spreadsheet problem

---

## 2. Monetization Model

**Free forever:**
- Core app (open repo, MIT or Apache 2.0)
- Self-hosting docs
- Public roadmap + weekly updates
- Community support (GitHub Discussions)

**Paid (pick 3 and stick to them):**

| Tier | What You Sell | Price Signal |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| Implementation pack | You adapt HalalFlow to their workflow | $500–$2,000 one-time |
| Hosted instance | Managed deployment, no self-hosting headache | $49–$149/month |
| Priority support | Setup, onboarding, migration call | $200–$500 flat |

**Rule:** Never sell code. Sell your time, your expertise, and their saved hours.

---

## 3. Three Best Paid Niches (Start Here)

**Niche 1 — Mosque Finance Admin**
- Pain: Treasurer managing donations, sadaqah, zakat, building fund in Excel
- They need: Receipt tracking, fund allocation, monthly reports for the community
- Why winnable: Mosques don't have IT teams. They'll pay for setup + hosted

**Niche 2 — NGO / Charity Zakat Operations**
- Pain: Collecting zakat, tracking disbursement, proving compliance to donors
- They need: Workflow that shows money came in → nisab checked → disbursed correctly
- Why winnable: Accountability pressure is high; transparency tools have real value

**Niche 3 — Muslim SME Invoicing & Expense Tracking**
- Pain: Small business owner who avoids riba-based tools (QuickBooks integrations, credit interest defaults)
- They need: Clean halal-first bookkeeping with murabaha/ijara tagging
- Why winnable: Underserved. No one talks to them directly.

---

## 4. What Stays Free vs. Paid

**Free (always):**
- Core data models (transaction types, zakat calculations, fund tracking)
- Basic dashboard
- CSV import/export
- Self-host Docker setup
- Documentation

**Paid (never open-sourced immediately):**
- Pre-configured niche packs (mosque template, NGO template, SME template)
- Custom integrations (accounting exports, bank feeds)
- White-labeled hosted instance
- Your onboarding time and setup calls
- "Done with you" implementation sprints

**Rule of thumb:** If it takes your time to implement, it's paid. If it runs itself, it's free.

## 5. Weekly LinkedIn Content Strategy

**Cadence:** 3 posts/week — no more, no less.

| Day | Post Type | Purpose |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| Monday | Build update — what shipped | Prove momentum |
| Wednesday | Problem/insight — what you learned | Build credibility |
| Friday | Community/question post | Drive engagement |

**Tone rules:**
- Write like a founder, not a marketer
- Show the actual problem before showing the solution
- Never use "excited to announce"
- Use numbers when you have them, honesty when you don't

---

## 6. 12-Week Content Plan

**Weeks 1–4: Foundation & Problem**
- W1: "Why I'm building HalalFlow" (the honest origin story)
- W2: Ship Week 1 update — first feature working
- W3: "What's actually broken in Islamic finance workflows" (problem post)
- W4: First demo screenshot + what it does/doesn't do yet

**Weeks 5–8: Build & Learn**
- W5: Ship zakat calculation module — show the math
- W6: "I talked to 3 mosque treasurers. Here's what they told me."
- W7: Ship mosque admin template — tag a few community accounts
- W8: "What I got wrong in week 1 (and what I changed)"

**Weeks 9–12: Traction & Conversion**
- W9: "HalalFlow is now live for self-hosting" — setup guide link
- W10: First user story (even if it's a beta tester)
- W11: "Here's what the paid customization includes" — direct offer post
- W12: 30-day retrospective — numbers, lessons, what's next

## 8. Landing Page Structure

**Above the fold:**
- Headline: "Islamic finance workflows, built in public"
- Subhead: "Open-source tool for mosques, NGOs, and Muslim-owned businesses. Free to self-host. Paid setup available."
- CTA 1: "View on GitHub" (primary)
- CTA 2: "Get it set up for me" (secondary — email capture or Cal.com link)

**Section 2 — The Problem:**
> Most Islamic finance operators are running critical workflows in spreadsheets. HalalFlow fixes that.

**Section 3 — What It Does:**
Three concrete features, not marketing language. Screenshots.

**Section 4 — Paid Options:**
Three cards: Implementation Pack / Hosted Version / Priority Support. Simple pricing. "Book a call" CTA.

**Section 5 — Built in Public:**
Link to GitHub, LinkedIn, latest weekly update. Prove it's alive.

**Footer CTA:**
> "Want HalalFlow configured for your workflow? [Book 20 minutes]"

## 9. Success Metrics

**Traction (weeks 1–8):**
- GitHub stars: 50 by week 4, 150 by week 8
- LinkedIn followers gained from build posts: 100+
- Inbound DMs or emails from potential users: 5+
- Repo forks or issues opened by non-you people: 3+

**Monetization (weeks 9–12):**
- Paid pilots confirmed: 1 minimum, 3 target
- Revenue: $300–$1,500 in first 90 days (signal, not scale)
- Conversations with mosque/NGO/SME operators: 5+
- Email list from landing page: 25+

**Health checks (ongoing):**
- Are you shipping something visible every week? (yes/no)
- Are posts getting comments from the target audience? (yes/no)
- Are people asking "how do I use this?" (yes = traction signal)

## 10. Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| No one knows it exists | LinkedIn consistency is non-negotiable for 12 weeks |
| Feature scope creep | Commit to 3 use cases only. Say no to everything else until week 13 |
| "Why would I pay when it's free?" | Because their time to set it up costs more than your fee |
| Community forks and no credit | MIT license is fine. Forks = validation. Chase the operator, not the developer |
| Burnout from shipping + posting | Posts take 20 minutes. Batch-write 3 at a time on Sunday |
