## HalalFlow: Open-Source Built-in-Public Strategy

---

### 1. Positioning

**One-liner:**
> "HalalFlow is the open-source workflow tool for operators running Islamic finance in the real world — not theory, not fintech buzzwords, just working systems."

**Frame it as a movement, not a product:**
- You're not selling software. You're building in public so practitioners finally have tools that match how they actually work.
- The repo is the proof of work. The posts are the credibility engine. The paid offer is the shortcut.

**Avoid:**
- "Islamic fintech ecosystem" (vague, feels like a pitch deck)
- "Sharia-compliant platform" (legal minefield, sounds corporate)

**Use instead:**
- "Built for people running halal money operations"
- "Practical tools for real Islamic finance workflows"

---

### 2. Monetization Model

**Principle:** The code is free. The implementation, specificity, and speed are paid.

| Layer | What | Price Signal |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Free | Core app, self-hosting docs, public roadmap | $0 |
| Paid Tier 1 | Custom implementation for your org | $500–$3k project |
| Paid Tier 2 | Niche use-case packs (pre-built, deployed) | $200–$800 |
| Paid Tier 3 | Hosted version (managed, no ops) | $49–$149/mo |
| Paid Tier 4 | Priority onboarding + setup | $300 flat |

**Start with Tier 1 and Tier 3 only.** Validate demand before building packs.

---

### 3. Three Best Paid Niches (First 90 Days)

**#1 — Mosque Finance Admin**
- Why: Every mosque has a treasurer, often using Excel or nothing. High trust required, low tolerance for generic tools.
- Pain: Tracking donations, Zakat funds, Sadaqah pools, expense approvals.
- Offer: "HalalFlow configured for your masjid in one week."

**#2 — NGO / Zakat Operators**
- Why: Zakat organizations handle designated funds with strict accountability. Compliance pressure is real.
- Pain: Fund segregation, disbursement tracking, donor reporting.
- Offer: "Zakat Operations Pack — fund tracking, disbursement workflow, donor receipts."

**#3 — Muslim SME Invoice + Expense Flow**
- Why: Small halal businesses (food, services, retail) need to track income and expenses without riba-based credit tracking baked in.
- Pain: Most accounting tools assume interest, credit lines, or Western financial structures.
- Offer: "HalalFlow adapted for your business — invoice flow, halal expense categorization, cashflow view."

---

### 4. What Stays Free vs. Paid

**Free (always):**
- Core app source code
- Self-hosting documentation
- Basic expense and income tracking
- Public roadmap and changelog
- Community Discord / GitHub Discussions

**Paid:**
- Implementation for your specific org
- Pre-configured niche packs (deployed + docs)
- Hosted version (you don't touch servers)
- Migration from your current system
- Priority support with SLA
- Private feature sponsorship (feature built for you, open-sourced after 90 days)

### 5. Weekly LinkedIn Strategy

**Rhythm:** 3 posts/week, every week, no exceptions.

| Day | Post Type | Goal |
|-----|-----------|------|
| Monday | "What I shipped" — concrete progress update | Credibility |
| Wednesday | "What I learned" — a real problem or insight | Trust + resonance |
| Friday | "Who this is for" — a use case, a persona, a story | Attract leads |

**Tone rules:**
- Write like a founder, not a marketer
- Use numbers and specifics ("tracked 847 transactions," not "powerful tracking")
- Show the work, not just the idea
- End with a question or a soft CTA, not a hard sell

### 10. Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Building in public with no audience | Start LinkedIn posts day 1, even with 50 followers. Consistency beats audience size early. |
| Scope creep killing momentum | Lock the core feature set for 90 days. No new categories without closing a paid deal first. |
| Niche too small | Mosques alone = thousands globally. NGOs add more. SMEs are massive. The niche is bigger than it feels. |
| "Why would I pay if it's free?" | You're not selling the code. You're selling the implementation, the hosting, the saved time. Frame it that way every time. |
| Credibility gap (no case studies yet) | Build in public = you are the case study. Your weekly posts ARE the proof of work. |
| Burnout from posting + building | Batch content on Sundays. 3 posts from one 90-minute session. Never improvise weekly. |

---

**The single most important thing:** Ship something visible every week and post about it. The repo is the product. The posts are the sales team. Start both on the same day.
