## HalalFlow — Open-Source Build-in-Public Strategy

---

### 1. Positioning

**One-liner:**
> "HalalFlow is an open-source workflow tool for Islamic finance operations — built in public, shaped by real users."

**What it's NOT:**
- Not "Islamic fintech ecosystem"
- Not "Sharia-compliant ERP"
- Not a bank, lending product, or compliance platform

**What it IS:**
- A practical ops tool for people who run halal money flows daily
- Built openly so operators can trust what's inside
- Payable when you want it faster, hosted, or custom

---

### 2. Monetization Model

```
FREE (builds trust + pipeline)         PAID (converts attention)
────────────────────────────────────   ──────────────────────────────────
Open-source repo                       Custom implementation
Self-hosting docs                      Niche use-case packs
Public roadmap                         Hosted version (no infra hassle)
Community Slack/Discord                Priority onboarding + support
Weekly build updates
```

**Pricing direction (don't publish until you have 1 paid deal to validate):**
- Hosted: $49–$149/mo per org
- Use-case pack: $500–$2,000 one-time
- Custom implementation: $2,000–$8,000 project-based

---

### 3. Three Best Paid Niches (Start Here)

**#1 — Mosque Finance Admin**
- Why: Clear pain, specific workflows (donations, Zakat distribution, payroll for imam), no good existing tool
- What you ship: Receipt tracking, monthly donation reports, Zakat disbursement log
- ICP: Mosque treasurer, Islamic center admin

**#2 — NGO/Charity Donation Tracking**
- Why: Islamic charities need donor tracking, fund segregation (Zakat vs Sadaqah vs Waqf), and audit trails
- What you ship: Multi-fund ledger, donor CRM lite, disbursement reports
- ICP: Islamic relief org ops manager, charity CFO

**#3 — Muslim SME Invoice & Expense Flow**
- Why: Business owners need Halal bookkeeping without riba-linked integrations; QuickBooks is overkill and Sharia-indifferent
- What you ship: Invoice templates, expense categorization (halal/flagged), simple P&L
- ICP: Muslim freelancer, small halal business owner, Muslim accountant

**Rule:** If it's generic infrastructure — open. If it requires domain knowledge or saves hours of setup — paid.

### 5. Weekly LinkedIn Strategy

**Posting cadence:** 3x/week

| Day | Post Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Build update ("shipped X this week") | Show momentum |
| Wed | Problem/insight ("here's what mosque treasurers actually deal with") | Attract ICP |
| Fri | Reflection or community ("what should HalalFlow solve next?") | Engagement, social proof |

**Voice:** Founder-builder. Direct. No buzzwords. Show the work.

**Not this:** "Excited to announce our journey toward disrupting Islamic fintech!"

**This:** "Spent 3 hours talking to a mosque treasurer today. He tracks 400 donations a month in a WhatsApp group. That's what I'm fixing."

---

### 10. Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Building features nobody uses | Talk to 5 real users before shipping month 2 |
| Open-source kills willingness to pay | Paid = implementation + time-saving, not locked code |
| Too broad (trying to serve everyone) | Pick ONE niche first — mosques — then expand |
| LinkedIn posts feel like marketing | Stay in builder voice; show actual work, not aspirations |
| Getting stuck in product, forgetting sales | Block 2 hrs/week minimum for outreach conversations |
| Competitor forks and "wins" | Community and reputation compound — they can copy code, not trust |

---

**The 30-day bet:**
Ship something visible every week. Have 5 real conversations. Close 1 deal — even $500.
That's proof of concept for the whole model.
