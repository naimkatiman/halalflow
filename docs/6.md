## HalalFlow: Open-Source Built-in-Public Strategy

---

### 1. Positioning

**One line:** HalalFlow is the open-source tool for Muslim operators who are tired of forcing generic finance software to work for Islamic workflows.

**Not:** "Islamic fintech platform" (vague, sounds like a startup pitch deck)
**Yes:** "We're building the workflow layer that Islamic finance operators actually need — in public, with you."

Credibility comes from shipping, not claiming. Every commit, every update, every real user conversation is content.

---

### 2. Monetization Model

| Layer | What | Price Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Free forever | Core app, self-hosting docs, community | $0 |
| Niche packs | Pre-built workflows for specific orgs | $200–800 one-time |
| Custom implementation | Adapt HalalFlow to your exact operation | $500–3,000 project |
| Hosted | Managed instance, no DevOps needed | $29–99/month |
| Priority onboarding | Setup + training call | $150–300 flat |

Rule: never paywalled features that make the core product less useful. Revenue gates *speed* and *specificity*, not functionality.

---

### 3. Three Best Paid Niches (First 90 Days)

**Niche 1 — Mosque Finance Admin**
- Pain: Mosque treasurers use spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and prayer to track donations, expenses, and zakat distribution
- Offer: HalalFlow configured for mosque budgets, Friday collection tracking, annual reporting
- Buyer: Mosque committee treasurer or admin
- Why now: Every mosque needs this; almost none have it

**Niche 2 — Zakat Operations Workflow**
- Pain: Zakat orgs manually reconcile who paid, who qualifies, distribution records
- Offer: End-to-end zakat intake → verification → disbursement workflow
- Buyer: NGO ops manager or Islamic charity director
- Why now: Ramadan creates urgency; compliance pressure is real

**Niche 3 — Muslim SME Invoice & Expense Flow**
- Pain: Small Muslim businesses need halal-compliant expense tracking (no riba, clear murabaha records) but QuickBooks doesn't care
- Offer: HalalFlow set up for their specific business type with Islamic finance tagging
- Buyer: Muslim business owner 5–50 employees
- Why now: Largest audience, highest volume of potential leads

---

### 4. Free vs. Paid — The Line

**Stays free (always):**
- Core app codebase
- Basic self-hosting guide
- Default workflows (invoice, expense, donation)
- Community forum / GitHub discussions
- Public roadmap

**Paid:**
- Pre-configured niche packs (mosque, zakat, SME)
- Your-specific-data migration
- Custom workflow design for your org
- Hosted version (no self-hosting effort)
- 1:1 onboarding and training
- Private feature sponsorship (shipped publicly after 60 days)

### 5. Weekly LinkedIn Strategy

**Format:** 3 posts per week, 3 types rotating:

| Type | Purpose | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Build update | Show progress, earn trust | 1x/week |
| Insight/observation | Position you as someone who understands Islamic finance ops | 1x/week |
| User/community story | Social proof, conversation starter | 1x/week |

Tone: direct, operator-to-operator. Not startup-pitch. Not motivational. Specific.

---

### 6. 12-Week Content Plan

**Weeks 1–4: Establish (Ship + Document)**
- W1: "Why I'm building HalalFlow in public" — origin story, the gap you saw
- W2: First feature shipped — screenshot, what it does, why it matters
- W3: Problem observation — "Every mosque I talked to is doing this in WhatsApp..."
- W4: Week 4 update — what shipped, what's next, one thing you learned

**Weeks 5–8: Attract (Niche + Community)**
- W5: "Mosque finance is broken" — specific post for niche 1
- W6: Zakat operations pain point post — no product pitch, just the problem
- W7: Show the mosque workflow pack — demo or walkthrough
- W8: "Here's what I got wrong in week 1" — transparency builds trust

**Weeks 9–12: Convert (Proof + Offer)**
- W9: First user/pilot story — what they struggled with, what changed
- W10: "Here's exactly what's free and what's not" — clear, no games
- W11: Roadmap post — show you're building for the long term
- W12: 90-day recap — numbers, lessons, what's coming

### 9. Success Metrics

**Traction (first 90 days):**
- GitHub stars: 100+ by week 12
- LinkedIn followers gained: 200+
- Inbound conversations started: 5+
- Repo forks or issues opened by non-you people: 3+

**Monetization:**
- Paid conversations: 5 by week 10
- Closed deals (any tier): 1 by week 12
- MRR target at 90 days: $300–500 (1-2 hosted or 1 custom)

**Content:**
- Post consistency: 3x/week, no missed weeks
- Avg post engagement rate: >3%
- DMs from target users (mosque/NGO/SME): 3+ by week 8

---

### 10. Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Build too slow, lose momentum | Ship something visible every week, even small |
| Too broad, no clear buyer | Lock the first 90 days to mosques only |
| Free product cannibalizes paid | Paid = speed + specificity, not features |
| LinkedIn posts feel like a startup pitch | Write operator-to-operator, not founder-to-VC |
| No users = no credibility | Find 2 mosques or NGOs to pilot before week 6 — even free |

---

**Bottom line:** The only thing that makes this work is shipping consistently and being specific about who you're helping. Mosques first. Zakat ops second. Everything else waits.
