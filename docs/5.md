## HalalFlow: Open-Source Built-in-Public Strategy

---

### Positioning

**Core message:**
> "HalalFlow is an open-source workflow tool for Islamic finance operations — built in public, by practitioners, for operators."

Not "platform." Not "ecosystem." A tool. For operators.

**Who it's for:** NGO finance leads, mosque treasurers, Islamic coop admins, Muslim SME owners managing halal cash flows.

**Why it resonates:** They're using Excel and WhatsApp. You're showing up with something purpose-built and transparent.

---

### Monetization Model

```
Free (open-source)          Paid (your time + expertise)
────────────────────        ────────────────────────────
Core app                    Custom implementation
Self-hosting docs           Niche use-case packs
Public roadmap              Hosted deployment
Community support           Priority onboarding / setup
```

**Rule:** Never sell the code. Sell the outcome of running it correctly for a specific context.

---

### 3 Best Paid Niches (prioritized)

**1. Mosque Finance Admin**
- High pain: manual tracking, volunteer turnover, donation reconciliation
- High trust barrier: they need someone who gets the context
- Low competition: nobody is targeting this
- Price point: $500–2,000 setup + optional monthly hosting

**2. NGO/Zakat Operations**
- Already have reporting requirements (donors, Shariah boards)
- Need audit trails and disbursement workflows
- Willing to pay for compliance-adjacent tools
- Price point: $1,500–5,000 custom implementation

**3. Muslim SME Invoice & Expense Flow**
- Largest addressable group
- Murabaha/installment tracking, halal supplier management
- Need something that "just works" without accounting software complexity
- Price point: $300–1,000 setup + hosted option

### Weekly LinkedIn Strategy

**Cadence:** 3 posts/week — no more.

| Day | Type | Purpose |
|-----|------|---------|
| Monday | Build update | Show progress, build credibility |
| Wednesday | Problem/insight | Attract operators who feel the pain |
| Friday | Community/reflection | Human connection, trust |

**Tone:** Practitioner talking to practitioners. Not marketing copy. No buzzwords. Short sentences. Real numbers when you have them.

---

### 12-Week Content Plan

**Weeks 1–4: Establish credibility**
- W1: "Why I'm building HalalFlow in public" — the origin story
- W2: First screenshot / demo of core feature working
- W3: "The problem with how mosques track donations today"
- W4: What the repo includes + first star milestone

**Weeks 5–8: Build audience around the problem**
- W5: Deep dive — Zakat calculation friction in NGOs
- W6: Behind the build — what broke this week and how you fixed it
- W7: "I talked to 3 mosque treasurers — here's what they said"
- W8: Ship a niche pack or significant feature publicly

**Weeks 9–12: Convert to conversations**
- W9: "HalalFlow now handles X — here's a walkthrough"
- W10: Case study or early user story (even informal)
- W11: "If you're running [niche], here's what it looks like for you"
- W12: Public recap — what shipped, what's next, how to work with you

### Success Metrics

**Traction (first 90 days)**
- GitHub stars: 100+
- LinkedIn followers gained: 200+
- DMs from target operators: 10+
- Conversations booked: 5

**Monetization (first 90 days)**
- Paid pilots or custom implementations: 1–2
- Revenue target: $1,000–3,000 (validation, not scale)
- Email list signups: 50+

**Health signals**
- Are people DM-ing you unsolicited? (Yes = positioning working)
- Are GitHub issues being filed by strangers? (Yes = adoption)
- Are people sharing your posts without being asked? (Yes = resonance)

---

### Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Build in public but nothing ships | Commit to one visible update per week — even small |
| Positioning too broad | Lock to 1–2 niches for the first 12 weeks |
| Free repo cannibalizes paid | It won't — buyers pay for implementation, not code |
| Audience won't pay for "Islamic" niche | Wrong framing — they pay for solving *their specific problem* |
| Burnout | No roadmap theater. Ship small. Post honest. |

---

**The one-line filter for every decision:**
> Does this help someone trust us, use the product, or pay us to implement it?

If not, skip it.
