## HalalFlow: Open-Source, Built in Public — Strategy Brief

---

### 1. Positioning

**The line:**
> "We're building HalalFlow in public — practical Islamic finance workflows for real operators, not enterprise decks."

Not: "Islamic fintech platform." That's vague and triggers procurement committees.

Target reader: A mosque treasurer, NGO finance lead, or Muslim SME owner who manages money manually in spreadsheets and feels the gap but can't afford custom software.

---

### 2. Monetization Model

```
Free core          →  attention, credibility, contributors
Paid layer         →  revenue without killing adoption
```

Three levers only:

| Offer | Price range | Who buys |
|---|---|---|
| Custom implementation | $500–$3,000 | SMEs, NGOs, cooperatives |
| Niche use-case packs | $97–$297 one-time | Self-hosters who want a head start |
| Hosted + onboarding | $49–$149/month | Non-technical operators |

No consulting retainers. No enterprise tiers. Not yet.

---

### 3. Three Best Paid Niches

**1. Zakat Operations Workflow**
- NGOs and mosque committees calculate and distribute Zakat manually every year
- Pain: tracking recipients, nisab calculations, audit trails for donors
- Pack: pre-configured workflow + reporting template + onboarding call

**2. Mosque Finance Admin**
- Weekly collections, expense approvals, committee sign-off
- Pain: no paper trail, no visibility for trustees
- Pack: role-based approval flow + monthly report template

**3. Muslim SME Invoice & Expense Flow (Murabaha-aware)**
- Small businesses need to flag riba-exposure in vendor terms
- Pain: standard accounting tools have no Islamic finance logic
- Pack: invoice templates + expense categorization + halal-status tagging

---

### 4. What Stays Free vs Paid

**Free (in the repo):**
- Core workflow engine
- Basic self-hosting docs
- Community templates
- Public roadmap
- Weekly build logs

**Paid:**
- Pre-configured niche packs (ready to deploy, not DIY)
- Hosted version (no infra headache)
- Migration from spreadsheets / QuickBooks
- Custom workflow buildout (your exact org, your rules)
- Private async support channel

Rule: **Free = the building blocks. Paid = the built thing.**

**Format: 3 posts per week**

| Day | Type | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Progress update | Show momentum |
| Wednesday | Problem/insight | Build credibility |
| Friday | Behind the scenes / ask | Build relationship |

Never: "Check out my startup." Never: feature lists.

### 10. Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Building in public burns momentum if nothing ships | Time-box features to 1 week max. Ship ugly, explain why. |
| Paid offer feels like bait-and-switch | Be explicit from day 1: core is free, setup is paid |
| Wrong niche (mosque finance has no budget) | Start with NGOs and Muslim SMEs — more budget, clearer ROI |
| Contributor PRs break your roadmap focus | Define scope in CONTRIBUTING.md. Decline gracefully. |
| LinkedIn posts get no traction | Tag 2–3 specific people per post. Ask for feedback, not likes. |

---

**The only rule that matters:**

Ship something visible every week. One working feature beats ten LinkedIn posts about vision.
